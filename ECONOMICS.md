# Rough SaaS Financial Assumptions

This document outlines our back-of-the-napkin financial assumptions and monetization models. Because this is an early-stage MVP, these figures represent loose benchmarks rather than absolute metrics.

---

## 📊 Loose Funnel Assumptions

Based on standard conversion rates observed across lightweight developer tools, we assume the following conversion flow:

* **Unique Visitors to Started Audits**: **~40% - 50%**
  * *Reasoning*: The minimal, clear value proposition and "no-registration" hook should capture about half of the landers.
* **Started Audits to Completed Audits**: **~75% - 85%**
  * *Reasoning*: Since the form has pre-filled pricing guesses and takes under 90 seconds, drop-off should be relatively low.
* **Completed Audits to Lead Capture (Playbook Submit)**: **~20% - 30%**
  * *Reasoning*: Unlocking standard negotiation templates and a structured checklist provides solid, direct value for founders trying to cut bills.
* **Leads to Booked Consultation Call**: **~5% - 10%**
  * *Reasoning*: Only founders with substantial monthly AI leaks (e.g. over $500/month in duplicate licenses) are likely to book a manual optimization session.

---

## 💵 Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) Guesses

Since our GTM relies entirely on organic developer channels, our blended CAC should be exceptionally low:

* **Community Threads (HN / Reddit)**: $0 direct ad spend. Costs only developer time to write case studies.
* **Co-Founder Share Loop**: Public share URLs dropped in Slack generate secondary conversions at $0 cost.
* **Blended CAC Guess**: **~$10 - $20 per lead**
  * *Reasoning*: Incorporates the server hosting costs, domain, and the developer time required to write case studies.

---

## 💰 Monetization Channels (The SaaS Guess)

If we were to commercialize this MVP, we assume two potential monetization pathways:

### 1. Simple Flat Platform Subscription
* **Model**: A monthly SaaS subscription ($49/month) for growing operations teams to monitor active developer seats, verify prompt caching triggers, and get automatic alerts when devs expense duplicate editor licenses.
* **LTV Assumption**: **~$600 per company**
  * *Calculation*: Assumes a conservative 12-month average retention period ($49 * 12).
  * *LTV-to-CAC Ratio*: Assuming a $15 blended CAC, this yields a highly viable **40x ratio**, proving that organic share loops can sustain lightweight developer SaaS tools.

### 2. Percentage of Savings Unlocked (Consulting Model)
* **Model**: Take a one-time 20% cut of the verified annual savings unlocked for teams on custom contracts.
* **Example**: A team with 15 users on ChatGPT Enterprise saves ~$30/user/month ($5,400/year total) by downgrading to Team. Taking a 20% cut yields a **$1,080 one-time fee**.
