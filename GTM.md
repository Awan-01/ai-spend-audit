# Go-To-Market (GTM) Plan: AI Spend Audit

This document outlines a simple, highly targeted roadmap to get this tool in front of early-stage founders and developers. Instead of trying to rank for expensive Google keywords, we focus entirely on organic, community-driven distribution.

---

## 🎯 Target Personas

We built this tool to target three distinct startup roles:

### 1. The Bootstrapped Founder / CEO
* **The Goal**: Keep burn rate low to extend their survival runway.
* **The Attitude**: Impatient. Doesn't want to sign up for bulky platforms or connect bank accounts just to check their spend.
* **Why they convert**: The 2-minute manual form needs no logins, is completely private, and outputs plan recommendations they can immediately act on.

### 2. The CTO / Tech Lead
* **The Goal**: Keep developer velocity high while avoiding billing clutter.
* **The Attitude**: Skewed towards developer speed. Annoyed when developers expense individual accounts on corporate cards without checking overlaps first.
* **Why they convert**: The engine is written by developers for developers. It highlights technical optimizations (like prompt caching and Cursor chat vs. browser tabs) that respect engineer speed.

### 3. The Operations Manager / Finance Lead
* **The Goal**: Clear up Expensify logs filled with scattered $20 subscription items.
* **Why they convert**: Gives them a clear, operationally sound rationale to bring to the CTO to standardise licensing.

---

## 🚀 Community Acquisition Channels

Rather than spending money on ads, we target places where founders actively discuss SaaS tools:

### 1. Y Combinator Ecosystem (Bookface & Batch Slacks)
* **Tactic**: Launching a dedicated thread on Bookface or Batch Slack groups.
* **The Hook**: *"We audited 15 early-stage stacks and found an average of $300-$500/month in duplicate licenses. Run your stack anonymously to check your burn."*
* **Why it works**: Direct peer referrals drive high-intent actions.

### 2. Hacker News & Reddit (r/startups, r/saas)
* **Tactic**: Write high-value, transparent developer logs rather than sales pitches. Share raw technical notes on how prompt caching cut a custom API bill by half, and link our audit tool as a free calculator.
* **Why it works**: Tech communities hate marketing hype. Providing honest technical guides is the easiest way to earn developer trust.

### 3. Organic Co-Founder Sharing (The Growth Loop)
* **Tactic**: When a founder runs an audit, the primary action is to copy the private, crawler-safe `/share/[id]` URL and drop it in their Slack channel with co-founders or tech leads.
* **The Hook**: The shared dashboard has no paywalls or sign-up gates, but includes a simple, clean button at the bottom: *"Analyze your own AI stack in 2 minutes"*. This organic share converts new users at zero cost.
